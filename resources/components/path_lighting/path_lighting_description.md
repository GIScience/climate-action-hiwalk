The map categorises paths based on their lighting.
Good lighting of paths increases actual and perceived safety when walking in the dark.
In contrast, missing or limited lighting may keep people from walking in the dark due to an uncomfortable feeling and safety concerns.

Explanation of the different path lighting categories from best to worst:
* Yes: Paths that are lit at least all night from evening until morning.
* Automatic: There are lights installed, but they only turn on when something passes by, usually through motion detection.
* Limited: There are lights installed, but they are not always on during the night.
* No: Paths without any lighting.
* Unknown: Paths for which there is no lighting information.

Data come from OpenStreetMap (OSM) and the categorisation is based on OSM lighting tags.
